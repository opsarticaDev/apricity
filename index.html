Brand Kit
The Name
+

There's a word from 1623 that almost no one uses anymore.

Apricity. The warmth of the sun in winter.

That moment when you're freezing - and then you feel it. Light breaking through. Heat where it shouldn't be. Warmth that finds you anyway.

We build apps for that feeling.

For the couple separated by oceans who light a candle at the same moment. For the pair stuck in routine who tear open an adventure and go. For the person who knows these years are short and wants to hold onto every sound, every quirk, every moment of this life they share.

Cold fades. Warmth stays.

And when warmth hits right, it doesn't ripple.

It makes waves.

The Tagline
+
Make waves. Not ripples.

This is our anchor. Our dare. Our demand - not of ourselves, but of the people who use what we build.

Ripples fade. Waves reshape.

We build technology that moves people - apps that create moments worth remembering, connections worth keeping, lives worth living.

Why Apricity
+

Apricity is felt. It's about warmth and presence. It's emotional.

Our apps aren't about understanding life more deeply. They're about feeling life more fully. Warmth, not analysis. Presence, not comprehension.

Apricity is the one word that matches what our apps actually do to people.

Why "Make Waves. Not Ripples."
+

Waves are force. Apricity is warmth.

This brand isn't just one or the other - it's both. We're not making cold, aggressive tech. We're not making soft, forgettable fluff. We're making warmth that moves people.

Apricity. Make waves. Not ripples.

Translation: We bring warmth - and that warmth changes things. It's not passive. It's not decorative. It hits.

The sun in winter doesn't just feel nice. It breaks through. It reaches you when you didn't expect it. That's not a ripple. That's a wave.

Visual Direction
+

Backgrounds

Dark and deep. No white. Think ocean at depth - the blues, the greens, the grays that shift depending on the light. The kind of color that feels like you're underwater looking up.

The Ocean Palette

The ocean isn't one color. It's:

  • Deep navy
  • Stormy gray-green
  • Teal at the edges
  • Slate blue
  • Almost black in the depths

All of these live in the palette. The ocean in all its moods.

The Sun

Warm gold breaking through. Not soft and pretty - persistent. The sun that finds you in the middle of the storm. Bright enough to cut through the dark. That moment of warmth when everything else is chaos.

The Feeling

Not a calm beach postcard. This is the sun shining through a storm. Hope in the middle of hard. Warmth that had to fight to reach you.

That's apricity exactly.

Logo Direction
+

Concept: Wave with Inner Glow

A real wave - fluid, powerful, in motion - with a warm glow held inside it.

Not abstract shapes suggesting movement. An actual wave. The kind you'd see cresting, curling, alive.

But not a postcard wave. Not a surf logo. This is a stormy ocean wave. Deep. Dark. The kind that has weight.

And inside it - caught in the curl, breaking through the water - light. Warmth. Gold cutting through the blue-green-gray.

The Image

A wave mid-curl. That moment right before it crashes. Inside the hollow of the curl, where it's normally dark and shadowed - that's where the light lives. Gold glowing from within. The sun found its way in.

The wave is the force. The light is the warmth. Together, they're Apricity.

Execution Notes

Element Requirement
Scale Must read as a wave at small sizes (app icon, favicon)
Balance Glow visible but not overpowering the wave
Palette Dark ocean colors with gold/amber warmth
Style Fluid lines, not stiff or geometric
Versatility Should work as single-color mark too
Shape No circular frame - the wave IS the logo shape

What It Says

The warmth doesn't exist despite the wave. The wave carries the warmth. They're not separate. Apricity and force are one.

Image Generation Prompt

"Logo design. A realistic ocean wave, mid-curl, powerful and dramatic. Side view showing the barrel forming as the wave crests. Not stylized. Not cartoonish. Not a spiral. A wave that looks like an actual ocean wave, simplified for logo use but maintaining realistic proportions and form. The wave is dark and stormy: deep navy, charcoal, slate, muted teal. Heavy. Moody. The kind of wave that has weight and force. Inside the barrel of the wave - the hollow tube where the wave curls over - golden light breaks through the water from behind. This is sunlight penetrating through the ocean, illuminating the thinner parts of the water from within. The effect should look like a photograph of a backlit wave, where the sun behind it makes the water glow gold and amber in the translucent areas. The light is not a separate element. It is the sun shining through the wave itself. Dark water on the thick parts, warm glow where the light penetrates through. Style: Sophisticated. Elegant. Painterly or photorealistic, not illustrated or flat. Minimal detail but realistic form. No hard outlines. Depth through color and light. Could hang in a gallery. Must scale to app icon size. No text. Symbol only. Dark charcoal or black background."
